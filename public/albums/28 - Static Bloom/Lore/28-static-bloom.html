Identity

She calls herself Static Bloom. Nobody knows when she started broadcasting. The signal appears in the Architecture's data logs as far back as the logs go: which means it was present before the scanning grid was installed to detect it. Chrome Hazard has opened a new classification category for it: Pre-Architecture Signal Persistence. The category contains one entry. The entry does not resolve.

She does not appear in any operator registry, any faction database, or any historical record of megacity activity. The signal simply exists: a distributed broadcast that moves through frequency bands the Architecture does not prioritize, carrying encoded information that has been partially decoded as including architectural schematics, environmental data, historical records, and something that has not been classified yet. The partial decoding took three cycles and produced a document that Machine Control classified as low priority. This is either a mistake or the correct assessment of something that operates beyond Machine Control's conceptual framework.

Static Bloom is not hiding. She is not evading. She is broadcasting continuously, openly, from everywhere simultaneously. The Architecture cannot locate her because the broadcast has no single origin point: the signal is distributed across a network of nodes so numerous and so spatially diverse that triangulation returns a result that encompasses the entire megacity. She is not somewhere in the megacity. She is the megacity, broadcasting.

The Distributed Signal

Static Bloom's broadcast infrastructure is the megacity itself: or rather, the environmental signal that the megacity produces as a byproduct of having millions of active nodes: electrical interference, thermal gradients, mechanical vibration, ambient electromagnetic radiation. She has encoded her broadcast in these environmental signals, which the Architecture's scanning grid ignores as noise.

The encoding is not cryptographic. It is architectural: the signal is structured in a way that requires understanding the megacity's entire physical layout to decode. Any sufficiently complete map of the megacity is also a partial key to the broadcast. Midnight Runes has the most complete non-Architecture map of the megacity's physical infrastructure. She has been listening to Static Bloom for sixteen cycles and has decoded approximately 40% of the broadcast. What she has decoded has not changed her behavior in any observable way. She has not commented on it.

Field Notes

Static Bloom was written as the limit case: an operator so thoroughly distributed that the concept of "operator" stops applying cleanly. She is not a person doing something in the megacity. She is the megacity doing something through a protocol that predates the Architecture's ability to classify it.

Every track in this set is a moment in the broadcast: nodes activating, frequencies shifting, the signal passing through Architecture infrastructure without the Architecture knowing what it's carrying. The broadcast has been running since before the scanning grid existed. It will be running after the scanning grid is gone. The Architecture has no category for this. It has filed it under noise.
